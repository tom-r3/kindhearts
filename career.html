Kind Hearts: A Service for Seniors

Home About Us Career

Thank you for your interest in Kind Hearts: A Service for Seniors. We are always interested is hearing from kindhearted, compassionate individuals who have a genuine respect for seniors.

We value our employees and offer a supportive environment with the opportunity for personal growth, a competitive salary, and flexible work hours. If you are interested in joining our team, please submit your resume to employment@kindhearts.ca.

Employment Opportunities

House Cleaner / Companion (Casual /Part Time)

The primary responsibility of this position is to assist seniors with the physical chores required to maintain a home. The main objective is to enhance the lives of our clients and to do so in a manner that is always courteous and respectful.

The House Cleaner/Companion performs general household duties relating to the upkeep of a home with the purpose of maintaining a safe, clean and healthy environment.

Duties

The duties of a house cleaner/companion may include, but are not limited to, the following:

  • Vacuum carpets; Vacuum and wash floors
  • Detailed dusting including baseboards and windowsills
  • Clean and sanitize all bathroom fixtures including tubs and showers
  • Clean kitchen counters, sinks, and appliances
  • Wash windows as needed
  • Empty wastebaskets
  • Change Linens
  • Do laundry including folding and ironing
  • Basic gardening
  • Shovel/Salt walkways
  • Ensure the safety and well-being of clients and report any concerns
  • Provide companionship through interaction and conversation

Qualifications

  • Experience working with seniors is essential and must be verifiable through references.
  • General house cleaning experience is required.
  • Candidates must be capable of demanding physical activity
  • Initiative, attention to detail and the ability to work unsupervised is necessary
  • Must exhibit mature, responsible behavior and understand the need for client confidentiality.
  • The ability to read, write, and speak English is required.
  • Must have reliable transportation to and from assignments.
  • A Police Information Check – Vulnerable Sector, must be provided.
  • First Aid and CPR training are definite assets.

Personal Support Worker/Companion (Casual/Part Time)

The primary responsibility of this position is to assist seniors in the community with their activities of daily living. The main objective is to enhance the lives of our clients and to do so in a manner that is always courteous and respectful and is conducive to independence, safety and privacy.

The Personal Support Worker/Companion works within the PSW scope of practice and displays professionalism at all times (competence, respect for others, compassion, positive attitude, acceptable language, professional appearance, discretion, confidentiality).

Duties

The duties of a PSW/Companion may include, but are not limited to, the following:

  • Travel to and from the clients’ home within an established/scheduled time period and work flexible hours to meet client needs.
  • As per the established individual care plan, provide assistance (and always involve the client) with ADLs: eating, bathing, grooming, skin care, dressing, and toileting.
  • Assist clients with limited mobility and always promote safety and physical comfort. This includes ambulation, mechanical or physical lifts/transfers, positioning, and range of motion activities.
  • Plan and/or prepare meals and snacks according to instructions and dietary requirements.
  • Provide support and assistance to other members of the health care team.
  • Maintain a safe, clean, and healthy environment and perform housekeeping tasks such as vacuuming, dusting, mopping floors, cleaning kitchens, tidying rooms, sanitizing bathrooms.
  • Assist with all aspects of personal hygiene including laundry related tasks.
  • Provide emotional and social support to the client and their families.
  • Offer companionship, encourage social outings, and promote stimulating activities including conversation, card/board games, letter writing, and reading.
  • Maintain records, document procedures, and observe/report condition and behavioral changes.
A Caregiver Must Not:
  • Administer or pour medication.
  • Cut the fingernails or toenails of any client.
  • Take clients in their car or drive clients in their own car.
  • Perform any type of heavy housework such as cleaning stoves with oven cleaners, moving heavy furniture, etc.

Qualifications

  • Must be a certified Personal Support Worker and have at least one year of experience working with seniors in a community or facility based environment.
  • General housekeeping and food handling/cooking knowledge is required.
  • Initiative, attention to detail, and the ability to work unsupervised (including problem solving skills) is necessary.
  • Must exhibit mature, responsible behavior and understand the need for client confidentiality.
  • Exceptional interpersonal skills and the ability to read, write, and speak English is required.
  • Must have reliable transportation to and from assignments.
  • A Police Information Check (Vulnerable Sector) must be provided.
  • Current First Aid/CPR certificate is required.
  • Knowledge and experience working with dementia clients, and Gentle Persuasive Approach training are definite assets.
